When deploying large language models (LLMs) locally, the primary bottleneck shifts from network latency to GPU memory bandwidth and capacity. This guide covers the essential hardware considerations for building an effective on-premise AI infrastructure.

## GPU Memory (VRAM) Sizing

To run models effectively, you must allocate sufficient VRAM. A typical rule of thumb is:

- **7B Parameters:** ~8GB VRAM (4-bit quantization)
- **13B Parameters:** ~16GB VRAM (4-bit quantization)
- **70B Parameters:** ~48GB+ VRAM (requires multiple GPUs)

By utilizing multi-GPU configurations, you can split the model layers across PCIe lanes, achieving the capacity required for enterprise-grade models without relying on cloud providers.

## PCIe Topology

Bandwidth between GPUs is critical. If your workload involves heavy tensor parallelism, you must ensure your server board supports dual x16 PCIe Gen 4 or Gen 5 lanes direct to the CPU.

> Choosing the right topology prevents bottlenecks during large batch inferencing.

## Storage Considerations

Models can be hundreds of gigabytes in size. We recommend NVMe storage pools in RAID 0 or RAID 10 to ensure that loading weights into VRAM happens as quickly as possible, minimizing downtime when switching between different models or agents.
